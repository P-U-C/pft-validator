# How to Earn on Post Fiat Network

The short version: Show up, do real work, build trust. Your access and earning power grow as your track record does.

---

## What is PFT?

PFT is earned — not mined. Every token represents verified work that moved the network forward. There is no free money here, and that's intentional. It means the tokens you earn are worth something.

---

## How earning works

PFT is distributed in monthly cycles called epochs. Each epoch has a fixed pool. You earn a share of that pool based on the quality and type of work you produce — not just the volume.

The pool is divided by weight, not by hours. A contributor who does one genuinely important piece of work can earn more than someone who produces a high volume of low-value completions.

There are three types of tasks, and they're not equal:

| Type | What it is | Relative value |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| Personal | Your own development and skill-building | Base |
| Network | Work that directly improves the network | 3× |
| Alpha | High-stakes strategic contributions | 9× |

Working your way up the task ladder is the fastest path to meaningful earnings.

---

## Your contributor level

Everyone starts at the bottom. Access and earning power expand as you build a track record.

### 🔵 New (Unknown)

You just joined. You can access basic tasks. Your earnings are held as pending credits until you establish yourself — think of it like a probationary period at a new job. Once you move up, your credits release.

### 🟡 Probationary

You've submitted work and the network is evaluating you. You can access more task types and a portion of your earnings pay out each cycle. The rest vest over 30 days — this protects the network and protects you (it signals that you're here for the long game, not a quick extraction).

Probationary lasts at least 14 days regardless of how much you produce. It's not a race. It's a track record.

### 🟢 Authorized 🏛

You've demonstrated alignment. Full access, full earnings, on-chain badge. This is the standard operating level for established contributors.

Getting here is a genuine milestone — it means the network trusts you and you're a recognized builder.

### 🌟 Trusted 🔑

Long-tenured, consistently aligned, identity-verified contributors. A small, meaningful group.

Trusted contributors can vouch for new contributors and participate in network governance. Hard to reach by design.

## What determines your weight?

Your share of each epoch's pool is based on:

- What you worked on — higher-tier tasks earn more weight
- How aligned your work is — does it actually advance the network's mission?
- Your track record — a growth index compounds over time. Consistent contributors earn more per unit of work than newcomers.
- Your contributor level — authorization tier applies a multiplier to everything else

## The Founder's Pledge

Before you receive your first task, you sign a short pledge with your wallet. It takes about 2 minutes to read. It's not a legal contract — it's a statement of intent.

Three things you're committing to:

1. Alignment first — your work should genuinely serve the network's mission, not just pass technical verification
2. Declare who you are — if you operate multiple wallets, disclose them. If you're a team or using automation, say so.
3. Engage, don't extract — the network can identify patterns that look like extraction rather than contribution, and it will respond

After the pledge, you'll see a one-screen summary of how you're evaluated, what can affect your earnings, and what your appeal options are. No surprises.

---

## Things to know

The network watches patterns, not just outputs. High-quality work that consistently advances the mission earns more over time. Optimizing for gaming metrics — technically valid but low-value completions — is visible and self-limiting.

Your track record compounds. Consistent contributors build a growth index that multiplies their epoch earnings. The longer you're genuinely contributing, the more efficient your earnings become.

No single contributor dominates. There's a cap on how much of any epoch a single contributor or group can capture. The network distributes broadly by design.

There's room for original thinking. A portion of each epoch's pool is reserved for work that doesn't fit neatly into the roadmap but is genuinely valuable. If you're doing something novel, flag it.

Authorization isn't the finish line. Authorized contributors are still evaluated continuously. The track record that got you here is what keeps you here.

## How to move up

**From New → Probationary:** Submit your first task. It's automatic.

**From Probationary → Authorized:** After at least 14 days and a minimum of 10 completed tasks, you can submit an authorization request. Include a brief description of your work, a few examples you're proud of, and a short statement about how you see yourself contributing to the network's growth. A team member reviews it within a week.

**From Authorized → Trusted:** This takes time — at least 90 days of consistent contribution, strong alignment scores, and nomination by existing Trusted contributors. Plus identity verification. It's meant to be rare.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I use multiple wallets?**
Yes, if you declare them. Undisclosed coordination between wallets is a violation of the Founder's Pledge and the network monitors for it.

**Can I use automation or AI tools?**
Yes. Declare it as part of your setup. Automated contributors have the same access path as individuals but are excluded from governance.

**What if I disagree with an authorization decision?**
You can submit a new request after 30 days with additional context. All decisions include a written explanation.

**What if my earnings are lower than I expected?**
Your epoch dashboard shows your current estimated allocation, the range, and the top factors affecting your weight. If something looks wrong, there's a review process.

**What happens if I stop contributing for a few months?**
Your track record doesn't disappear, but your growth index decays during extended inactivity. Coming back after a break means rebuilding some momentum — but your authorization level is preserved.

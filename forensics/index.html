Funding topology — the circular pattern

Of the Botfather's 706 direct funders:

  • 676 (95.7%) received their original PFT from the Botfather itself before later sending some back
  • Only 30 funders have no prior Botfather origination trace in the visible window
  • The first 10 indexed inbound transactions are all from zero-memo wallets with small amounts (52–323 PFT)

This is not an inflow of external capital: it is a cycling pattern. The Botfather distributes 100 PFT seed amounts to fresh wallet addresses, which subsequently return smaller variable amounts to the Botfather. The behavioral signature is consistent with wash-trading or consolidation laundering, not with reward accumulation from productive work.

Behavioral fingerprint

  • Payment amount range: 5.67 PFT – 1,151.37 PFT (median 131.90 PFT)
  • Memo usage: 0%

  • Hourly activity spread: 22 of 24 UTC hours (no sleep pattern)

3 · Classification methodology

Bot candidates are classified by deterministic on-chain behavioral signals. Three confidence bands are defined and all queries in this report use the HIGH band unless otherwise noted.

BandCriteriaCount
HIGH0 memos, 10+ txns, balance ~15 PFT712
MEDIUM0 memos, 10+ txns738
LOW0 memos, >0 txns764

Rationale — why these signals identify bots

  • Zero memo participation is inconsistent with any human use pattern on the Post Fiat network, where task completion, verification, and reward all flow through memos.
  • Balance at exactly the XRPL minimum reserve (~15 PFT) across 10+ transactions indicates the wallet was activated with minimum capital and never retained value. Real contributors accumulate PFT from rewards.
  • Sustained transaction activity (10+ txns) distinguishes bots from dormant wallets and new accounts.

The HIGH-band intersection of all three signals produces 712 wallets. False-positive risk is low: it would require a real contributor to bulk-transfer PFT across many transactions while never using memos and never accumulating more than ~15 PFT, which has not been observed among any of the 68 known contributors.

5 · Reward leakage verdict

Verdict
NO LEAKAGE DETECTED WITHIN VISIBLE WINDOW
Query Q2 returned zero matches when tested against all 7 infrastructure wallets × 712 high-confidence bots. No direct reward transaction from task node, airdrop, or distribution wallets landed on any zero-memo wallet with bot signature. The network integrity guarantee holds at the corrected 712-bot scale.

Interpretation: the bot operation is capitalized from outside the visible reward flow. The ~88,279 PFT of pre-window capital that seeded the Botfather may have originated from a non-task source (direct faucet, early allocation, or off-chain transfer) but never from the task reward path we can observe.

6 · Limitations

Ledger window

The postfiatd archive node operates with online_delete retention. The indexed window (ledger 1,343,459 – 1,926,688, spanning 47 days) represents the furthest back our crawler could reliably fetch via account_tx RPC at the time of investigation. Any reward leakage or capital injection before 2026-02-26 is outside the scope of this report.

Pre-window capital origin

The 88,279 PFT implied by the Botfather's balance-minus-net-flow accounting cannot be traced to its original source using the data available to this investigation. A future deeper crawl (pulling from NuDB archive rather than account_tx) could extend the visible window.

Classification false-negative risk

The HIGH-band filter excludes 26 wallets that have zero memos and 10+ transactions but hold slightly more than ~15 PFT (these fall into the MEDIUM band, not HIGH). These are likely active bots mid-cycle when the snapshot was taken.

Operator identity

The Botfather is a funding vertex — infrastructure, not a person. Linking the 712-wallet operation to a real-world identity is not possible from public ledger data alone. This report makes no claim about who runs the operation, only about what the operation does on-chain.

Single-operator assumption

The 712 wallets are attributed to a single operator based on the shared funding origin (Botfather) and near-identical behavioral signatures. Multi-operator collaboration or stolen-key scenarios are possible but not evidenced in the data.
